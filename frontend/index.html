Why Early Vision Screening Matters

Early screening helps detect potential vision risks before they impact daily life, supporting timely professional care and better long-term outcomes for people of all ages.

Early Risk Awareness

Many vision problems develop gradually without obvious symptoms. Regular screening helps identify changes sooner, giving you time to take action before they affect your daily activities.

Better Treatment Outcomes

When potential issues are caught early, you can seek professional care at the right time. Early intervention often leads to more effective treatment options and better visual outcomes.

Accessible Preventive Care

Digital screening tools make basic vision checks easier to access — whether at home, in assisted-living settings, or in community health environments where clinical resources may be limited.

Our Process

How Our Vision Screening Works

Our platform uses two complementary screening approaches — AI-powered image analysis and a functional Landolt C test — to provide a well-rounded view of your visual health.

1

Eye Image Screening

Capture an image of your eye through the device camera. The system analyzes visual features to identify potential risk indicators. This is a screening support tool — not a medical diagnosis.

2

Landolt C Vision Test

Identify the direction of the gap in the Landolt C symbol following guided voice and on-screen instructions. This evaluates visual clarity and functional vision performance at various acuity levels.

3

Screening Summary

After completing both screenings, the platform generates a simple report with your results and personalized recommendations on whether professional consultation may be helpful.
