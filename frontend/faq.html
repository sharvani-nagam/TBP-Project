Clear Answers to Eye Health Care Questions

  • Dry Eye: Redness, irritation, or a gritty sensation
  • Glaucoma: Gradual vision loss or blind spots
  • Cataracts: Cloudy or blurred vision
  • Eye Strain: Headaches and difficulty focusing

Dry eye syndrome occurs when your eyes don't produce enough tears or when tears evaporate too quickly. This can cause discomfort, redness, and a gritty sensation in your eyes.

Adults should have a comprehensive eye exam every 1-2 years, or more frequently if you have existing vision problems, diabetes, or a family history of eye disease.

Common signs include gradual vision loss, blind spots in peripheral vision, increased eye pressure, and in severe cases, eye pain or headaches. Regular eye exams are crucial for early detection.

Cataracts often develop due to aging, prolonged UV exposure, medical conditions like diabetes, smoking, or certain medications. They cause clouding of the eye's natural lens.

While screens don't cause permanent damage, prolonged use can lead to digital eye strain, causing discomfort, headaches, and dry eyes. Follow the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds.

Children should have their first eye exam at 6 months old, again at 3 years, and before starting school. Regular exams help detect vision problems early.

Yes, contact lenses are safe for daily wear if you follow proper hygiene practices, replace them as recommended, and have regular check-ups with your eye care professional.

Optometrists provide primary vision care including eye exams and prescriptions. Ophthalmologists are medical doctors who can perform eye surgeries and treat complex eye diseases.

Common signs include frequent squinting, headaches (especially after reading), blurry vision at any distance, eye strain, and difficulty seeing at night. Schedule an eye exam if you experience these symptoms.
